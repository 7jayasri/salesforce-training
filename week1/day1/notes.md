# Day 1 Notes - Salesforce Basics

## What is Salesforce?
Salesforce is a cloud-based CRM platform used by companies to manage customers, sales, support, and business processes.
It helps organizations store customer data in one place and improve communication and workflow management.

## What is CRM?
CRM stands for Customer Relationship Management.
It is a system used to manage customer information, track interactions, improve customer service, and increase sales efficiency.
Example:
Companies like Amazon or banks use CRM systems to manage customer details and support requests.


## Why Companies Use Salesforce
- Centralized customer data
- Better communication between teams
- Sales tracking
- Customer support management
- Workflow automation
- Cloud accessibility

## Salesforce Basics
### Lead
A potential customer who may become a buyer in the future.

### Contact
An individual customer or person associated with a company.

### Account
A company or organization associated with customers.

### Opportunity
A potential sales deal.


## Salesforce Data Structure

### Object
An Object is similar to a table in a database.
Examples:
- Account
- Contact
- Student

### Record
A single entry inside an object.
Example:
One student’s details inside Student Object.
### Field
A field stores specific information inside a record.
Examples:
- Name
- Phone Number
- Email


## Admin vs Developer

### Salesforce Admin
- Configures Salesforce using built-in tools
- Manages users and reports
- Works mostly without coding

### Salesforce Developer
- Builds custom applications
- Writes Apex code and LWC
- Creates integrations and automation

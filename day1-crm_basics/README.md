# Salesforce CRM Basics – Day 1

## 1. What is CRM?
CRM stands for Customer Relationship Management.
It is a system used by companies to manage customer information, sales, communication, and business processes in one place.
CRM helps businesses improve customer relationships and increase sales efficiency.

---

## 2. Why Companies Use Salesforce
Companies use Salesforce because it helps them:
- Store customer data securely
- Track sales and opportunities
- Improve communication with customers
- Automate business processes
- Increase productivity
- Manage customer support efficiently
Salesforce is cloud-based, so teams can access it from anywhere.

---

## 3. Salesforce Objects

### Account
An Account represents a company or organization.
Example:
- College
- Hospital
- Business company

---
### Contact
A Contact represents a person associated with an account.
Example:
- Student
- Patient
- Employee

---

### Opportunity
An Opportunity represents a potential business deal or transaction.

Example:
- Student admission
- Patient treatment package
- Event booking

---
## 4. Real-World Mapping (College Admission System)
### Lead
A student interested in joining the college.
### Account
The college or department.
### Contact
The student or parent.
### Opportunity
The admission process for a course.
### Customer
The student who successfully joins the college.

---
## 5. Business Flow
Lead → Contact → Opportunity → Customer
1. A person first shows interest (Lead).
2. Their details are stored as a Contact.
3. The business opportunity is tracked.
4. Once completed successfully, the person becomes a customer.
